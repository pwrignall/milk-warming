Physics model: The water volume is chosen so the system reaches thermal equilibrium at 37.5°C — meaning the milk physically cannot exceed that temperature, however long you wait. The starting water temperature is derived from the law of conservation of energy: heat lost by water = heat gained by milk. Warming time uses Newton's law of cooling with a two-body exchange model, estimating the bottle's heat transfer from its surface area. Assumes a standard glass baby bottle at fridge temperature (4°C).
